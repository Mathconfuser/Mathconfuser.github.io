    <div class="publication-abstract">
      <p>
        This paper studies principal specializations of beta-Grothendieck polynomials through pattern occurrences in permutations. For permutations avoiding the pattern 1423, the authors prove a nonnegative formula for the principal specialization in terms of certain pattern counts. Using an inverse conservation principle, they also obtain a corresponding nonnegative expansion for permutations avoiding 1342. These results partially resolve conjectures of Gao, Gaetz, Mészáros--Tanjaya, and Dennin. The proof is based on a reduction algorithm for the classical pipe dream model.
