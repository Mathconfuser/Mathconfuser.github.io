    <div class="publication-abstract" hidden>
      <p>
        We study a problem on principal specialization of Grothendieck polynomials motivated by Stanley's work on Schubert shenanigans. In the case of permutations $w$ avoiding the pattern $1423$, we prove a nonnegative formula of the principal specialization of the $\beta$-Grothendieck polynomial $\mathfrak{G}_w^\beta$ in terms of the number of occurrences of certain patterns in $w$. Applying the inverse conservation principle, we also obtain a nonnegative formula in the case of permutations $w$ avoiding the pattern $1342$. This settles part of a conjecture of Gao, Gaetz, Mészáros--Tanjaya and Dennin. Our proof is based on a reduction algorithm on the classical pipe dream model of $\beta$-Grothendieck polynomials.
